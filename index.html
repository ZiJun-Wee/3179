World efforts to reduce carbon emissions.

With global warming becoming an increasingly pressing issue in the world today, this visualisation aims to put into perspective how are we doing currently.

Most current global CO2 Emissions

The choropleth map on the right portrays the latest CO2 emissions per capita from 2023. As seen from the projection, the distribution of CO2 per capita is not evenly distributed across different countries and different regions.

Relationship between non-renewable forms of energy and CO2

There is a positive correlation between the use of non-renewable forms of energy(such as fossil fuels) and the CO2 emissions. However, income level does not seem to have a relationship with the level of CO2 emissions, despite the countries having the means to pursue better ways to produce energy and reduce their CO2 emissions.

Global energy consumption breakdown

There is a noticeable trend towards a growing share of energy consumption derived from low-carbon sources. Renewable energy and natural gas consumption have experienced a significant increase, while the use of energy generated from petroleum, coal, and similar fossil fuels has declined. However, it's important to note that despite these positive shifts, the majority of global energy consumption still originates from non low-carbon methods of production

Varying efforts to move away from non-renewable forms of energy

There is an effort by countries in the higher income groups to move away from non-renewable forms of energy. Seen on left, we can see that there is a slight upwards trend in the share of renewable energy in the total energy consumption for every income group except for the low income group.

This is reinforced by how the efforts to move towards low carbon electricity has been led by higher income countries. Low carbon electricity is classified as electricity produced from renewable sources or nuclear plants. As seen in the the visualisation, the countries in the upper-middle and high income group have moved towards switching to low carbon electricity while the countries in the lower income groups seem to be moving away from the low carbon energy.
